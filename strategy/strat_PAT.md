# PAT Strategy, relevant sections for ESAT Maths and Physics

Scope reviewed: PAT papers available in the project folder, 2017 to 2024 specimen, with solutions.  
Use PAT selectively. It is not a pure multiple-choice ESAT-style paper and has longer written problems, but it is excellent for deeper Physics and mathematical modelling.

---

## 1. Format and timing

PAT 2023 example:
- 2 hours.
- 26 questions.
- 100 marks.
- Mixture of multiple-choice and written-response questions.
- Working can receive partial credit.
- No formula sheet or calculator.
- Candidates are advised to divide time according to marks.

ESAT-style use:

| PAT question type | Use for ESAT? | How |
|---|---|---|
| 2-mark multiple choice | High | timed quick drills |
| short 3 to 4 mark physics | High | method fluency |
| 5 to 7 mark modelling | Medium | untimed technique review |
| long proof/calculus | Low | extension only |
| optics/waves/mechanics/circuits | High | targeted topic practice |

Suggested timing:
- 2-mark MCQ: 2 minutes maximum.
- 3 to 4 mark short Physics: 4 to 6 minutes.
- 5 to 7 mark modelling: review slowly after ESAT core work.

## 2. What to use from PAT

High relevance:
- Mechanics and energy.
- Dimensional analysis.
- Circuits and transformers.
- Springs and SHM-style energy.
- Waves, optics and interference.
- Kinematics and circular motion.
- Probability/counting where short.
- Function transformations and sketching.

Lower priority:
- Long written derivations.
- Advanced orbital mechanics if outside ESAT.
- Multi-part optics derivations unless used as challenge review.
- Heavy calculus questions.

## 3. PAT quick-technique map

| PAT question type | Fast technique |
|---|---|
| Equal kinetic energy | Use scaling, not full arithmetic |
| Order-of-magnitude astrophysics | period ≈ length/speed, frequency = 1/period |
| Pulley displacement | Count supporting rope segments |
| Function transformation | Track vertex and sign |
| Circuit symmetry | Identify equal potentials/currents |
| Dimensional analysis | Solve powers of base units |
| Springs | Energy stored = 1/2 kx² |
| Drag/equilibrium | Resolve forces, use tan θ |
| Optics/refraction | Snell's law and limiting cases |
| Waves | Period, particle travel and phase |
| Probability with dice | Enumerate outcomes or use symmetry |
| Sketching curves | stationary points, intercepts, end behaviour |

## 4. Year-by-year strategy notes

### PAT 2017

Pattern:
- Useful for mechanics, electricity, waves and problem-solving discipline.
- Older PAT style contains longer written reasoning, so select short questions first.

Best techniques:
- Use units and scaling for quick MCQs.
- For mechanics, draw free-body diagrams.
- For circuits, use symmetry and equivalent resistance.
- For waves, separate wave speed from oscillation of particles.
- For long questions, identify the governing conservation law first.

Priority:
- Short mechanics/electricity/waves questions.
- Avoid long derivations until after ESAT speed base is built.

---

### PAT 2018

Pattern:
- Strong on mechanics and mathematical modelling.
- Useful for energy, momentum, graphs and estimation.

Best techniques:
- Use conservation of energy before force equations where possible.
- For circular motion, compare required centripetal force with available force.
- For graphs, label intercepts, gradients and limits.
- For estimation, keep one significant figure until final step.

Priority:
- Use as a targeted Physics extension set.

---

### PAT 2019

Pattern:
- Useful mix of mechanics, circuits, waves and mathematical functions.
- Some questions are too long for direct ESAT timing but excellent for technique review.

Best techniques:
- Break long questions into small physical statements.
- Use dimensional checks early.
- For circuits, calculate equivalent resistance or use current conservation.
- For optics/waves, draw rays or wavefronts before formulae.

Priority:
- Short MCQ and first parts of long questions.

---

### PAT 2020

Pattern:
- Good for non-calculator fluency and physics reasoning.
- Contains many questions where the fastest route is conservation, scaling or dimensional analysis.

Best techniques:
- Use energy methods for motion under gravity.
- Use proportionality for scaling questions.
- Use limiting cases to check formulae.
- Use a clean sign convention for momentum/collisions.

Priority:
- Mechanics and electricity sections are high value.

---

### PAT 2021

Pattern:
- Good for advanced ESAT-style Physics enrichment.
- Useful for forces, circuits, waves and graph sketching.

Best techniques:
- For force problems, draw all forces and choose axes.
- For electrical power, choose `P = IV`, `I²R`, or `V²/R` based on knowns.
- For graph sketches, determine asymptotes and stationary points before drawing.
- For multiple-part questions, harvest early marks first.

Priority:
- First half of the paper and short physics questions.

---

### PAT 2022

Pattern:
- Useful for mathematical physics and multi-step modelling.
- Strong for energy, waves, optics and mechanics.

Best techniques:
- Use conservation first.
- Use dimensional analysis for unfamiliar formulae.
- In optics, draw normal and ray path.
- For waves, identify phase, path difference and wavelength.
- For long algebra, define variables cleanly and avoid expanding too early.

Priority:
- Use as advanced topic practice, not timed ESAT mock.

---

### PAT 2023

Pattern:
- 26 questions, 100 marks, 2 hours.
- The paper starts with short multiple-choice questions and then moves into longer written problems.
- High-value ESAT-relevant items include kinetic energy scaling, probability chains, order-of-magnitude frequency, pulley displacement, transformations, circuit symmetry, stopping/circular force comparison, transformer current, dimensional analysis, springs, drag/equilibrium, exponential equation, total internal reflection, trig equation, waves, graph sketching and dice probabilities.

Best techniques:
- Q1 style: equal kinetic energy means `m1v1² = m2v2²`; solve by square-root scaling.
- Q3 style: frequency estimate = speed / length scale.
- Q5 style: pulley displacement by rope-segment counting.
- Q8 style: circuit symmetry can beat calculation.
- Q10 style: stopping force and centripetal force can be compared using energy.
- Q12 style: ideal transformer: power conservation and turns ratio.
- Q16 style: dimensional analysis with base units.
- Q17 style: equilibrium with drag: horizontal/vertical force ratio gives tan θ.
- Q20/Q23 style: optics requires a clean ray diagram and limiting cases.
- Q24 style: wave period = wavelength/speed; particle travel per period = 4A.
- Q26 style: dice probabilities by outcome counting.

Priority questions:
- Q1 to Q12 as timed quick drill.
- Q15, Q16, Q17, Q20, Q21, Q23, Q24 and Q26 as slower technique review.
- Q18, Q22 and Q25 are useful but more extension-style.

---

### PAT 2024 Specimen

Pattern:
- Modernised specimen is useful for expected current PAT style.
- Prioritise short questions and any multiple-choice reasoning that overlaps with ESAT.

Best techniques:
- Treat it as a topic-mapping exercise.
- Extract mechanics, waves, circuits, energy and functions.
- Do not use long written marks as ESAT timing calibration.

Priority:
- Shorter items first.
- Use longer written questions only for depth after NSAA/ENGAA/TMUA speed work.

## 5. Best PAT strategies

### Strategy 1: Marks guide time

PAT gives marks; ESAT does not. For PAT practice:
- 2 marks: quick method, no long derivation.
- 4 marks: one main idea plus calculation.
- 6 to 7 marks: multi-step modelling, use only for depth.

### Strategy 2: Dimensional analysis is a major tool

PAT often rewards building a formula from units.

Process:
1. Write target units.
2. Write units of each variable.
3. Solve powers.
4. Check limiting behaviour.

### Strategy 3: Conservation first

Before using equations of motion:
- energy
- momentum
- charge/current
- power
- angular/circular constraint

### Strategy 4: Draw physical diagrams

For PAT, a diagram is often the solution:
- free-body diagram
- ray diagram
- circuit reduction
- wave path
- pulley rope segments
- graph sketch

### Strategy 5: Harvest partial technique

Even if a PAT question is too long for ESAT:
- take the first idea
- record the governing principle
- convert it into an ESAT shortcut
